About

Ethical capitalism by design

TilNeeded, LLC is a Public Benefit Corporation. Fairness in a TilNeeded Contract is not a mission statement — it is enforced by the contract's own architecture: both parties join voluntarily and benefit simultaneously; the hell-or-high-water obligation binds both equally; every term is publicly visible; no transfer occurs without mutual approval; and no middle layer extracts value from either side.

The system requires no subsidies or mandates. Factories earn more than distributor channels pay them. Buyers pay less than distributor channels charge them. Waste, emissions, and needless spending fall as inherent properties of the contract rather than as add-on programs.

Founder

Rhonda Roland Shearer

Rhonda Roland Shearer — CEO of TilNeeded, LLC and inventor of the TilNeeded Contract — is a sculptor, scholar, and award-winning investigative journalist. She is director of the Art Science Research Laboratory, the nonprofit she co-founded in 1998 with her late husband, Harvard professor Stephen Jay Gould.

Her supply chain work is grounded in crisis operations, not theory. After September 11, 2001, she and her daughter led WTC Ground Zero Relief, supplying millions of dollars of free safety equipment to first responders through July 2002. In March 2020, her Cut Red Tape 4 Heroes project distributed more than 25 million items of free PPE to New York City hospital workers, first responders, and community organizations — direct experience of the allocation cancellations, hidden pricing, and counterfeit-quality failures that TilNeeded Contracts are engineered to eliminate.

TilNeeded's advisory board brings four decades of healthcare supply chain leadership, global logistics and trade experience, and structured finance expertise.
