Who it serves

Three parties. One transparent contract.

The same mechanism serves buyers who need certainty, factories that need reliable revenue, and agencies accountable to the public for both.

Government agencies

Preparedness without the warehouse

Agencies stockpile at 130% — full purchase price plus a year of carrying costs — then discard expired product and buy again. An RCR Election secures the same readiness for 10%, with the expiration clock starting only at delivery. Virtual stockpiles cannot expire in a warehouse.

The Make PPE in America Act directs DHS, HHS, and the VA to sustain domestic PPE manufacturing. TilNeeded Contracts route agency demand directly to American factories — a compliance path that costs less than the status quo rather than more. The contract structure aligns with the Federal Acquisition Regulation's core principles: full and open competition, fair and reasonable pricing, and transparent terms, with every contract publicly searchable.

American factories

Two revenue streams instead of one

Factories in distributor channels earn thin margins on product alone. A TilNeeded factory earns product revenue at better-than-distributor pricing and RCR Fee income on reserved capacity — cash flow that arrives whether or not purchases are triggered, smoothing the demand cycles that hollow out American manufacturing. Capacity matching guarantees a factory never commits beyond what it can deliver.

In the two-year pilot, RCR Fee cash flow helped an American PPE factory cover payroll and plan hiring — production capacity sustained by reservations rather than speculation about future orders.

End-user buyers

Certainty at a tenth of the cost

Hospitals, institutions, and businesses hold TilNeeded Contracts instead of inventory: price, quantity, and delivery speed locked with the factory itself, immune to allocation cancellations, with no NDA concealing what anyone pays. If needs change, the Transfer Provision recovers value with mutual approval. If a shortage hits, the contract performs — that is what the pilot's 100% record means.
