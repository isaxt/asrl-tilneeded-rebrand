How it works

The TilNeeded Contract, step by step

A TilNeeded Contract is bipartite: factory and end-user buyer, nothing in between. Removing the middle layer creates a dual pricing advantage — the factory earns more per unit than distributor channels pay, and the buyer pays less than distributor channels charge, at the same time.

Step one

Choose an election mode

Buy Now (BN)

Purchase immediately

Factory-direct pricing with secured delivery terms. Suited to products needed today.

Reserve Capacity Reservation (RCR)

Reserve now, buy when needed

Pay a negotiated RCR Fee — 10% of the purchase price is the starting point; every term is negotiable — to hold the factory's verified capacity at locked price, quantity, and delivery speed, typically for twelve to twenty-four months.

The RCR Fee is not a prepayment. It is income the factory earns for accepting an unconditional, first-in-line delivery obligation — payment for reserve value that has always existed in manufacturing capacity but was never systematically available for purchase.

Step two

Trigger the purchase — or don't

If the buyer triggers the purchase during the term, the factory receives 100% of the contract price in addition to the RCR Fee, and delivers at the locked terms — 30 days, 90 days, whatever the contract specifies. If the buyer never triggers, the contract expires: the factory keeps the RCR Fee, and the buyer has paid 10% for a year of secured supply instead of committing roughly 130% — the full purchase price plus a year of inventory carrying costs, which industry benchmarks place at 20–30% of inventory value annually.

The binding core

Mutual hell-or-high-water performance

Once a purchase is triggered, both parties must perform, regardless of market conditions — no force majeure exceptions beyond those required by law, no unilateral cancellation. In typical commercial practice such clauses bind only the buyer; the TilNeeded Contract binds both parties equally. Standard commercial remedies under UCC Article 2 — damages, cover, and specific performance — apply directly between buyer and factory, with no intermediary in the chain of accountability.

Overbooking is impossible by design

Capacity matching

RCR reservations are sold only up to the factory's verified, fulfillable capacity within each delivery-speed tier. Delivery speed is the underwriting variable: a factory's 90-day tier can commit roughly three times its monthly production, and reservations are capped accordingly. Partners receive monthly capacity reports. A factory cannot sell reservations it cannot honor.

Factories can also hold TilNeeded Contracts on their own inputs — reserving raw-material capacity upstream — extending the same certainty through the production chain. Three factories each holding a 30% reservation deliver more resilient redundancy than one buyer carrying 130% of inventory three times over.

When needs change

The RCR Transfer Provision

A buyer whose needs change does not cancel — cancellation does not exist in a TilNeeded Contract. Instead, the contract can be transferred to another qualified buyer, structured as an assignment under UCC § 2-210, with the mutual approval of both original parties. The factory's obligations remain exactly as written; the original buyer and the factory share the proceeds of the transfer under pre-agreed terms. The RCR Fee becomes recoverable value rather than a sunk expense — and the factory's commitment stays intact for whoever holds the contract.

Inherent, not added on

Carbon and waste reduction built into the contract

Production, shipping, and warehousing happen only when a purchase is triggered. Deferred delivery defers emissions; a never-triggered contract eliminates them — no production run, no freight, no warehouse, no expired product in a landfill. The decision not to buy is financially accounted for at signing, and that same decision reduces or eliminates the transaction's carbon footprint. The expiration clock on dated products starts at delivery, not at stockpiling.
